Best Destinations from Palermo by Private Yacht

Egadi Islands

The Egadi archipelago — Favignana, Levanzo and Marettimo — lies 30–40 nautical miles west of Palermo. These are among the cleanest and least developed islands in Italy. Favignana is known for its flat, transparent lagoons; Levanzo for its prehistoric cave paintings and intimate bays; Marettimo for its wild, mountainous landscape and exceptional diving conditions.

Capo Gallo Marine Reserve

Just north of Mondello, Capo Gallo is a protected headland with dramatic limestone cliffs, underwater caves and crystal-clear water. By private boat you can access anchorages and swimming spots that are inaccessible from land — a 30-minute departure from Palermo harbour.

Ustica

Ustica is a volcanic island 52 km north of Palermo, entirely surrounded by a marine reserve. The underwater visibility here is exceptional — up to 40 metres in calm conditions. Private boat trips allow flexible timing and anchoring at the best spots.

FAQ — Yacht Charter Palermo

Do I need a boat licence to charter a yacht from Palermo?

No. All charters arranged by Le Sicilien include a licensed professional skipper. You simply enjoy the boat.

Can I request specific destinations or routes?

Yes. Our concierge team customises every itinerary. Tell us your interests — beaches, diving, ruins, fishing — and we design the route accordingly.

Is food included on the charter?

Catering can be added on request. We can provide on-board picnic boxes with Sicilian produce, or arrange a full lunch prepared by a private chef before departure.

When is the best season for yacht charter in Palermo?

May to October are the ideal months. July and August are peak season — the sea is warmest. May, June and September often provide the best conditions with lighter winds and calmer water.
